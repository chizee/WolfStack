# Welcome to Level 2

You finished Getting Started. That's not a small thing — most people never get a server doing useful work *and* backed up *and* watched. You did.

So what's Level 2 for? In Getting Started you learned the **six core things** every setup needs. Level 2 is the **doors** — the bigger features you skipped on purpose: linking servers together, running full virtual machines, growing your storage, automating chores, and putting an AI assistant to work. None of them are required. All of them are useful *when you have a reason*.

## The method hasn't changed

You already know how to learn WolfStack: **one screen, one task, at a time.** Level 2 is exactly the same, just with rooms you haven't been in yet. Nothing here is harder — it's only newer.

## How Level 2 is laid out

Each lesson opens one door:

- **WolfNet** — make two servers behave like they're on the same network.
- **Virtual machines** — run a whole separate computer when a container isn't enough.
- **Storage** — mount remote disks and outgrow a single drive.
- **WolfFlow** — automate the boring, repetitive stuff.
- **WolfAgents** — a named AI assistant that knows your setup.

Take them in order, or jump straight to the one you have a real need for today. Both are fine.

> **The golden rule still holds:** open a door because you have a *specific* reason — "I want my two sites to talk" → WolfNet — not because it's there. You spent Getting Started learning to avoid overwhelm. Keep that habit. It's the difference between an operator and someone drowning in tabs.

## ✓ What you just learned

- Level 2 covers the **optional power features**, one door per lesson.
- The method is identical: **one task at a time**, driven by a **real need**.
- You can take the lessons in order or jump to the one you need now.
