# You're an operator now

Look at what you can do now. You started not sure what a server even *was*. Now you can run apps, build containers and full VMs, link machines across the internet with WolfNet, grow your storage, automate your chores, and put an AI assistant to work — and you can keep it all backed up, monitored, and locked down.

That's not "someone who followed a tutorial." That's an **operator**.

## What you've actually unlocked

Between Getting Started and Level 2, you've opened every door that matters for running a real setup:

- **Run things** — App Store, Docker, LXC, VMs.
- **Connect things** — WolfNet across locations.
- **Keep things** — backups, off-box storage, restores.
- **Watch things** — Issues, alerts, status pages.
- **Lock things down** — login hardening, server hardening.
- **Scale yourself** — WolfFlow automation and WolfAgents.

## The doors still left

There are a few rooms you haven't needed yet — and that's fine, that's the whole philosophy. When a real need shows up, they're waiting:

- **Fleet Security** and **Fleet Logs** — once you run *several* servers and want one security and log screen for all of them.
- **Tenants** and **Cluster Browser** — when you manage setups for *other people* and want one dashboard across them.
- **Status Pages** — a public uptime page, for when others depend on your service.
- **Storage Arrays**, **XO Pools**, **Databases** — specialist tools for when the specialist need arrives.

You already know how to learn each one: open it the day you have a reason, one screen at a time.

> **The only rule that ever mattered:** open a door because you have a *real, specific need* — never out of curiosity-overload. That single habit is what keeps a growing setup calm instead of chaotic. It's served you through two whole courses. Keep it for good.

## ✓ What you just learned

- You've covered **every core operational skill** WolfStack offers — running, connecting, keeping, watching, securing, and scaling.
- The remaining features (**Fleet**, **Tenants**, **Status Pages**, specialist storage) are **need-driven** — learn them when a real reason arrives.
- The operator's habit: **one door at a time, driven by need.**

## Where to go from here

Nothing. Seriously — go *run* your setup for a while. The best next lesson is real use: let a backup run, watch an alert fire, fix one small thing. Come back to open a new door when your setup gives you a reason to. You've got the method, and you've got this. 🐺
