# How to get unstuck

Everyone gets stuck — it's not a sign you're doing it wrong, it's just part of running servers. What separates people who keep going from people who give up isn't talent; it's knowing where to turn. Here's your list.

## Where to turn, in order

1. **Come back to this course.** The compass (bottom-left) is always here. Re-reading a lesson when you're actually doing the task — instead of just reading ahead — is when it really clicks.

2. **Use the built-in AI assistant.** WolfStack ships with an AI agent that knows the platform. Set it up under **Settings → AI Agent** (add a provider), then just *ask it* — "how do I move a container to another node?", "why won't this VM get an IP?". It can read your setup and give specific answers.

3. **The Issues and Predictive Inbox pages.** Before you go hunting, check whether WolfStack already noticed the problem — and maybe offers a one-click fix. Often the answer is sitting right there.

4. **Help & Docs.** The question-mark button (bottom-left) opens the official documentation, with deeper reference for when you want the full detail on a feature.

5. **The community and support.** When you're truly stuck on something specific, reach out — describe what you did, what you expected, and what actually happened. A clear question gets a fast answer.

## How to ask a good question

You'll get help faster if you can say three things:

- **What you did** — the exact steps.
- **What you expected** to happen.
- **What actually happened** — including any error text (you can copy it from the task log or terminal).

That's it. You don't need to diagnose it yourself; you just need to describe it clearly.

## One last thing

You started this course because WolfStack felt like too much. Look at what you can do now: stand up an app, build a container, open a shell, take and schedule backups, and get alerted when something breaks. That's not "a beginner poking around" — that's **running your own infrastructure.**

The rest of WolfStack will still be there when you want it, one door at a time. You've got the method now. Go build something.

## ✓ What you just learned

- When stuck: **revisit a lesson → ask the AI agent → check Issues/Inbox → Help & Docs → community/support.**
- A good question states **what you did, expected, and got** (with the error text).
- You've gone from overwhelmed to operating your own servers. That's the whole point — well done.
