# The only six things you need at first

People feel overwhelmed by WolfStack because they assume they have to understand *all* of it. You don't. Here is the entire list of things that matter when you're starting out. Six things. That's the whole syllabus for this course.

## The six things

1. **Your server** — the machine WolfStack runs on. You'll learn to find it and look inside it.
2. **Installing an app** — getting something useful running, the easy way, from the App Store.
3. **A terminal** — opening a command line on your server or inside an app, when you need one.
4. **Backups** — making a copy so you can sleep at night.
5. **The Issues page** — your 30-second morning check for "is anything wrong?"
6. **Getting help** — knowing where to turn when you're stuck (including coming back here).

That's it. Master those six and you are a competent WolfStack operator. Everything else — virtual machines, mesh networking, automation, AI agents — is *optional power* you can reach for later, one piece at a time, when you have an actual reason to.

## Why this works

Most "I gave up" moments come from trying to learn a tool by reading every screen at once. That never works, for anyone, with any software. The fix is to learn it the way you'd learn a new town: one street at a time, starting from your front door.

> **A promise:** we will never introduce two new things at once. Every lesson does exactly one thing, then stops. If a lesson ever feels like it's piling on, that's a bug in the lesson, not a gap in you.

## You can stop any time

After almost every lesson from here on, you'll have something real and working. If you do the App Store lesson and then walk away for a month — great, you've got an app running and you're already getting value. Come back to the compass whenever you want the next piece.

## ✓ What you just learned

- There are only **six things** that matter at the start: your server, installing an app, a terminal, backups, the Issues page, and getting help.
- Everything else is optional and can wait until you have a reason for it.
- Each lesson teaches exactly one thing — you're never expected to hold it all in your head.
